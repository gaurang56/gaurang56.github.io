About Me

Hi, I'm Gaurang, a Computer Science undergraduate at Northeastern University, graduating in 2027. I'm deeply interested in machine learning, product development, and real-world impact. Over the past few years I've had the chance to work across industry and research, and each experience has shaped how I think about building things that matter. I genuinely believe that the most exciting work happens when you combine strong technical foundations with a clear understanding of the people you're building for.

On the industry side, I've interned at companies like Etsy, Scale AI, and Cognizant, where I worked on everything from content moderation systems operating at massive scale to validation frameworks for large language models. These experiences taught me how to ship reliable software under real constraints, tight deadlines, ambiguous requirements, and production environments where mistakes have consequences. I learned to appreciate the difference between code that works in a notebook and code that works for millions of users.

On the research side, I hold an AI and ML Research Fellowship at MIT, where I work on computational pathology in collaboration with Novartis. My work focuses on making advanced pathology tools more accessible for example, achieving strong classification accuracy using frozen foundation model pipelines that dramatically reduce training time and cost. I love the challenge of translating cutting-edge ML architectures into practical tools that clinicians and scientists can actually use.

Outside of academics and work, I'm an entrepreneur at heart. I've co-founded consumer products like AloAngels, an AI-powered VC matching platform, and Mood2Music. I'm always thinking about the next thing to build, whether that's a new app, an open-source tool, or a creative side project that might resonate with people. When I'm not coding, you'll probably find me traveling between Boston and Doha, exploring new cities, or brainstorming product ideas with friends.
