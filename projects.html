Projects & Research

A selection of things I've built and worked on.

I've always been drawn to projects that sit at the boundary between research and product. Whether it's building a machine learning pipeline for medical imaging, launching a consumer app, or hacking together a prototype over a weekend, I find the most energy in work that has a clear path from idea to impact. Below are some of the projects and research threads that I'm most proud of, each one taught me something different about how to approach hard problems.

Computational Pathology Research (MIT & Novartis)

My primary research focus is on democratizing computational pathology. Working under an AI/ML Research Fellowship at MIT, I developed pipelines using foundation models like Virchow2 and Kaiko Midnight to detect mitotic figures in histopathology images. The key result: we achieved 84% classification accuracy with a 961x training speedup compared to traditional fine-tuning approaches. We also demonstrated cross-species generalization, training on human tissue and successfully transferring to canine samples. This work is being prepared for submission to MICCAI and arXiv, with co-authors from multiple Boston-area institutions.

AloAngels AI-Powered VC Matching

AloAngels is a consumer platform I co-founded that uses AI to match startup founders with the right venture capital investors. The idea came from watching how inefficient the fundraising process is for early-stage founders, most spend weeks cold-emailing investors who aren't even a good fit. We built a matching engine that considers investment thesis, stage preference, sector focus, and geographic alignment to surface the most relevant connections. Building AloAngels taught me a lot about product-market fit, user onboarding, and the gap between a working demo and something people actually want to use every day.
